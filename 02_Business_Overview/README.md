# Business Overview

## Procurement Challenges

Procurement in many organizations is managed through manual approvals, email communication and spreadsheet tracking. These methods result in fragmented workflows and limited control over procurement activities.

As multiple stakeholders are involved and financial commitments are made at each stage, the absence of a structured system increases the likelihood of delays, errors and non-compliance.

---

## Problem Statement

The existing procurement process lacks standardization and system-driven control. This leads to:

- Delayed approvals due to manual routing  
- Limited visibility into request and order status  
- Vendor comparison conducted outside the system  
- Late identification of invoice discrepancies  
- Payment delays due to manual reconciliation  
- Incomplete audit traceability  

These issues impact efficiency, financial accuracy, and governance.

---

## Business Objectives

The system is designed to address these challenges by:

- Reducing approval and procurement cycle time  
- Minimizing manual errors through system-driven validation  
- Enforcing compliance through structured approval workflows  
- Providing visibility across all procurement stages  
- Strengthening financial control and audit traceability  

---

## Scope

The system covers the complete Procure-to-Pay lifecycle:

- Purchase Request creation and approval  
- RFQ initiation and vendor interaction  
- Vendor evaluation and selection  
- Purchase Order creation and approval  
- Goods receipt and inspection  
- Invoice validation and payment processing  

Each stage is connected through defined workflows with clear ownership and validation rules.

---

## Stakeholders

The system involves the following roles:

- Requester: Initiates purchase requests  
- Manager (Level 1 and Level 2): Approves requests based on cost thresholds  
- Purchase Team: Manages RFQ, vendor selection and PO creation  
- Purchase Manager: Reviews and approves Purchase Orders  
- Vendor: Provides quotations and fulfills orders  
- Warehouse Team: Handles goods receipt and inspection  
- Accounts Team: Manages invoice processing and payments  

---

## Success Criteria

The system is considered effective when it achieves:

- Faster approval and processing timelines  
- Reduction in manual errors and discrepancies  
- Improved visibility across procurement stages  
- Accurate financial validation before payment  
- Complete traceability of procurement activities
